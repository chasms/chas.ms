1959

Barrett Strong

In 1959, Motown-then under the name of founder Barry Gordy's first record label, Tamla Records-released Barrett Strong's "Money (That's What I Want)". Having co-written the song with Janie Bradford, the Motown boss knew he had a hit on his hands and leased it to Anna Records for international distribution. Anna Records was a Detroit-based company that was owned by Berry's sisters Anna and Gwen Gordy and Billy Davis. The label operated from 1958 to 1961, when it was absorbed into Motown. "Money" was a hit, reaching the #23 position, but more importantly, Barrett Strong joined Motown as a staff songwriter. He stayed with Motown until 1973.
