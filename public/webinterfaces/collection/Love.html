1962

The Contours

In 1962,"Do You Love Me? (Now That I Can Dance) was released under the Gordy Records label, another subsidiary of Motown. Written and produced by Motown CEO Berry Gordy "Do You Love Me?" was the Contours' only Top 40 single on the Billboard Hot 100 chart in the United States. Notably, the record achieved this feat twice, once in 1962 and again in 1988, when Alvin and the Chipmunks covered it. The main point of the song is to name the Mashed Potato, The Twist, and many other fad dances of the 1960s. The song is also noted for its false ending at 2:26.
